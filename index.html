Multisource report and personal testimony

A documented crisis, and one life fighting to survive.

Based on reports from The New York Times, The Washington Post, BBC, Iran International — and my own experience.

Chapter 1 — A global crisis and a personal story

Major media — The New York Times, The Washington Post, BBC, Iran International — documented forced deportations of Iranians from the United States to Iran: fear, coercion, and severe human rights risks.

For me, Mehrdad Delir, this is not distant news. It mirrors what I survived.

Chapter 2 — The New York Times: Flights turned into nightmares

Scenes of crying, pleading, handcuffs, leg irons, and deep fear. Many had lived for years in the U.S., with pending asylum cases and families. Not routine — a moment when dignity was crushed.

Chapter 3 — The Washington Post: Returning to a country without safety

Hidden political arrangements and severe consequences: detention, interrogation, persecution upon arrival in Iran. Real lives at stake.

Chapter 4 — BBC: Fear, confusion, and an uncertain future

Many didn’t understand why they were detained or removed. Some were chained; some fainted. Families were in shock. Serious risks awaited them in Iran.

Chapter 5 — Iran International: The September 29 flight — and my story

At the moment of transfer, overwhelmed by terror, I cut my wrist to resist being forced aboard. Officers simply bandaged the wound and forced me onto the plane to Tehran. That broke my life — proof my life meant nothing to those in power.

Chapter 6 — My current reality: Fighting to stay alive

I am in Iran — not in safety, not in peace, but in crisis. Under pursuit, threatened, hiding, unable to work or leave legally, danger growing closer every day.

Chapter 7 — My appeal: Help me survive

To escape, I’m asked to pay very high amounts for risky routes — far beyond my means. Please be my lifeline. Help me stay alive.
